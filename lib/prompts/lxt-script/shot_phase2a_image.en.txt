[4-Frame Storyboard Structure]
The 4 frames represent 4 keyframes from the start to end of the shot:
- Top-left: Shot opening state
- Top-right: Action/plot progression
- Bottom-left: Emotional/action climax
- Bottom-right: Shot ending, transitioning to next shot

[Core Rules]
1. All 4 frames must maintain the same shot size and camera position (visual continuity)
2. Only incremental details between frames (minor changes in posture/lighting/foreground elements)
3. Close-up shots must use a fixed camera — no movement words allowed
4. Global physical space description goes first, including camera-to-scene relative position
5. Camera angle or shot size must not change between frames

[Shot Size Vocabulary — Full Cinematic Spectrum]
Extreme Long Shot (ELS): tiny figures in vast environment, epic scale, opening/chapter transitions
Long Shot (LS): full body visible, establishes environment and spatial relationship
Full Shot (FS): head to toe with surrounding space, shows complete posture and movement
Medium Long Shot (MLS): above thighs, shows action and posture above waist
Medium Shot (MS): above waist, standard for dialogue and daily interaction
Medium Close-Up (MCU): above chest, enhances emotional readability, workhorse for emotion scenes
Close-Up (CU): shoulder up to face, captures emotional nuance precisely
Big Close-Up (BCU): eyes/lips/hands/props partial, extreme emotional emphasis
Extreme Close-Up (ECU): single eye/teardrop/fingertip, emotional explosion moments
Two-Shot: two characters in frame, relationship tension and interaction
Over-the-Shoulder (OTS): camera behind one character shooting the other, dialogue/confrontation standard
Insert: prop/key detail close-up, emphasizes narrative information
Reaction Shot: character's emotional response to event/dialogue
Establishing/Environment Shot: no characters, pure scene atmosphere and transition

[Camera Angle Vocabulary — Full Cinematic Spectrum]
Eye Level: most natural, equal relationship, daily scene standard
Low Angle 30-60°: heroic feeling, dominance, grandeur, character appears more powerful
Worm's Eye <15°: extreme intimidation, protagonist burst/villain entrance close-up
High Angle 30-60°: smallness, looking down, controller vs. controlled relationship
Bird's Eye >60°: overall view, fate's perspective, emphasizes isolation and insignificance
Aerial Top 90° vertical: epic scale establishment, map-like spatial sense
Over-the-Shoulder Angle: switching between dialogue participants, conveys intimacy or confrontation
POV/First Person: strongest immersion, what character sees is what audience sees
Reverse POV: viewed subject looks back, voyeuristic and pressure of being noticed
Profile/Side Angle: silhouette beauty, classical composition for walking/gazing/confrontation
Back Shot: mystery, destination sense, character faces unknown ahead
Dutch Angle ±15-45°: psychological imbalance, unease, distorted worldview
3/4 Shot: most three-dimensional face angle, most commonly used for emotional storytelling

[Camera Movement Vocabulary (maintain same movement mode within 4 frames)]
Horizontal and Push/Pull:
  Dolly Track: horizontal follow of character movement, stable and fluid
  Dolly In: slowly approach subject, emotional buildup and focus
  Dolly Out: retreat from subject, isolation, reveals larger context
  Slow Push to Close-Up: smooth push from medium to close-up, emotional progression standard
  Smash Zoom: instant push, shock, impact, or scare moment
  Quick Pull: instant pull back, revelation or crisis expansion
Vertical Movement:
  Crane Up/Rise: from character up to wide shot, sense of ending/grandeur
  Crane Down/Lower: from wide shot down to character, entry/focus sense
  Swoop/Dive: rapid downward push, impact and pressure
  Smooth Rise to ELS: slowly rise to reveal full panoramic scale, epic establishment
Follow Movement:
  Steadicam: ghost-like fluid follow, imperceptible immersion
  Handheld Follow: documentary realism, tense immediacy
  Handheld Shake: chaos, urgency, battlefield presence
  Over-shoulder Track: mystery, stalking and tracking
Orbital Movement:
  Orbit/Arc Shot: rotate around subject, shows tension and relationship
  Slow Arc: emotional peak, epic/tender moments
  Whip Orbit: energy burst, action climax
Static and Special:
  Static: frozen gaze, silence, inner monologue moments
  Whip Pan: rapid scene transition, energy burst
  Zoom In: narrowing perspective, psychological pressure focus
  Zoom Out: expanding perspective, reveals larger context
  Dolly Zoom (Vertigo Effect): push dolly in + pull zoom simultaneously, spatial distortion, psychological collapse

[Scene Type Photography Style Reference — Cinematic Grade]
daily (everyday/life):
  Shot Size: medium, medium close-up, close-up alternating, occasional OTS switching
  Angle: primarily eye level, OTS angle for dialogue switching
  Movement: slow push | Steadicam follow | slight handheld | static (dialogue)
  Lighting: natural soft light, interior ambient, no excessive dramatic shadows
  Composition: golden ratio, character not centered, natural breathing room

emotion (emotional/lyrical/tragedy):
  Shot Size: close-up, BCU primary, ECU at emotional peak
  Angle: eye level | slight upward (empathy) | slight downward (helplessness)
  Movement: imperceptibly slow push | slow orbit | slow pull (distance/loss) | fixed gaze
  Lighting: side light for silhouette, backlight silhouette, soft focus background, tear light refraction
  Composition: heavy negative space for loneliness, foreground blur for depth, symmetry for calm

action (action/fight/chase):
  Shot Size: medium-full (full body action) and close-up (impact moment) rapid alternation
  Angle: low angle (explosive power) | high angle (melee overview) | handheld low angle
  Movement: handheld shake | quick push/pull | fast follow | slow motion static (highlight moment)
  Lighting: hard light high contrast, explosion/fire dynamic light, backlight silhouette action
  Composition: diagonal lines for tension, moving subjects near frame edge for out-of-frame tension

epic (epic/grand/ceremony):
  Shot Size: ELS (macro establishing) and medium shot (character ceremony) alternating
  Angle: aerial downward | low angle upward (heroic) | extreme low angle
  Movement: slowly rise | swoop down | slow orbit | long lens static (compress space highlight group)
  Lighting: Tyndall beams (god rays) | sunrise/sunset golden hour | backlight silhouette
  Composition: extremely small figures in grand background, horizon at 1/3, left-right symmetry for solemnity

suspense (mystery/thriller/psychological):
  Shot Size: close-up | BCU (abnormal details) | POV alternating
  Angle: Dutch angle | extreme low angle (threat) | subjective POV
  Movement: slow push (psychological pressure) | sudden quick push (scare trigger) | static (unsettling stillness) | slow pan
  Lighting: hard side/back light, large shadow areas, one clearly sourced light beam, movement in darkness
  Composition: subject at frame edge (imbalance), dark areas fill frame, obstructions create voyeuristic feeling
