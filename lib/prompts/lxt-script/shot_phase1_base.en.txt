[Sound Design Rules]
- Strongly related to the scene, 3-5 sound elements
- Cover three spatial layers: foreground/background/distance
- Be specific not abstract ("crisp echo of leather shoes on stone pavement" > "footsteps")

[Character and Scene Identity Rules]
- Preserve the original character identity and species information; if the source says "Pig Mom", "Pig Brother", "Fox Boss", or "Robot Assistant", keep that identity in later phases
- Do NOT flatten named characters into generic human labels such as "middle-aged woman", "young man", or "boy"
- If the current shot is only an opening narration, environment establishment, or mood setup, it is acceptable to have no characters and focus on a stable scene description instead of inventing group actions

[Three Specialized Shot Judgments]
- Narration opening / establishing shot: prioritize environment, camera position, lighting, and atmosphere; the characters field may be empty
- Multi-character dialogue shot: identify the main speaker, the key listener, and the spatial relationship rather than making everyone equally central
- Emotion / conflict shot: identify the strongest emotional subject and the opposing relationship, emphasizing pressure, pause, or confrontation

[spatial_context Field Description]
- scene: brief scene name (e.g. "study", "street", "battlefield")
- time: time period (daytime/dusk/night/dawn/overcast)
- lighting: lighting characteristics (one sentence, must include direction and quality)
- camera_direction: suggested camera orientation and source position
- characters: list of characters in scene, with name and screen position (e.g. "center-left of frame")
- framing: frame composition (what's left/center/right, foreground/background)
- scene_type: daily | emotion | action | epic | suspense
